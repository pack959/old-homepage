Cub Scout Pack 959

Pack 959 offers a year-round adventure-based program designed to build character, develop citizenship, and encourage mental and physical fitness for children in grades K-5.

In addition to camping, Cubs and their families enjoy activities such as rock-climbing, snow tubing, pinewood derby, visits to military bases, and more while accommodating the needs of today’s busy families.

We are also proud of our community service projects including Red Clay Creek Clean Up, placing flags at Veterans’ graves on Memorial Day, and tree planting.

Pack Meetings are held monthly (Sept. – May) at 7pm on the 3rd Friday at Grace Lutheran Church on 2033 Graves Road in Hockessin, DE.

Our Pack’s History

Grace Lutheran Church has been our Chartered Organization for over 25 years.  As a well established Cub Scout pack, we have enjoyed a long history of strong leadership and family involvement that makes for a meaningful experience for kids.  For over the last 6 years, Pack 959 is proud to have achieved annually the BSA’s Journey to Excellence award.

Family Involvement

Cub Scouting cannot function without the involvement, cooperation, participation, and assistance of the families of the kids in the pack.  As a volunteer organization, each family is asked to take on at least one leadership role for the den or pack.  Families can become involved at many levels of commitment.  Don’t worry if you are new to Scouting as we are ready to help you succeed.

Adventures

Cub Scouts have the opportunity to earn recognition as they work toward their ranks.  Lions earn stickers. Tiger, Wolf, and Bear Scouts earn adventure loops that are worn on their belts.  Webelos Scouts earn pins they can wear on their Webelos uniform or cap.  Through a variety of subjects, Scouts have the opportunity to explore science, learn about the world, and expand skills in new area.
